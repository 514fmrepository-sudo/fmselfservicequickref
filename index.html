Frequently Asked Questions

Log into milConnect at milconnect.dmdc.osd.mil. Finance office does not process SGLI changes.

Finance does not automatically update your records when you make changes in DEERS (such as getting married). DEERS and Finance are separate systems. To update your dependency status for BAH, you must take the following steps:

Submit Required Forms

  • AF Form 594 (required for all dependency changes).
  • Long Tour Orders (over 30 days): AF Form 594 is also required to verify dependency and housing requirements. You must include a copy of your certified start orders, and the start date should account for travel days.
  • If claiming a secondary dependent, also complete the appropriate DD Form 137. Forms and instructions are available on the DFAS Secondary Dependency page.

Provide Documentation

  • For marriage or primary dependent updates: submit original marriage documentation along with AF Form 594.
  • For secondary dependents: provide proof of the dependent's income (e.g., pay stubs, Social Security statements, W-2s) and proof of your financial support if they do not live with you (e.g., canceled checks, money orders, receipts, or support allotment).
  • For submission: submit DAF 594 form with supporting documentation (marriage certificate, birth certificates, divorce decree, etc.) to 514amw.fmfp@us.af.mil.

Reference Guides

  • If you're unsure what documents are needed or how to complete AF Form 594, refer to the BAH Recertification Guide for step-by-step instructions.

Note: Update your address with FSS first. Submitting paperwork for a secondary dependent does not increase your BAH rate if you already have a primary dependent, but it may affect eligibility for other entitlements (ID card, medical benefits, etc.).

Finance cannot provide tax advice. For W-2 issues, contact DFAS directly. For general military pay questions, use 514amw.fmfp@us.af.mil.

Garnishments are court-ordered deductions. Finance cannot start, change, or stop these. For questions or changes, contact your state agency (payment questions/changes) or DFAS - child support alimony (if not reflecting).

Debts usually show in the remarks section of the LES where the debt is posted. If you don't see a reason, please submit a query through myPay.

Common causes of debts and how to avoid them:

  • Order changes -- Turn in order modifications (especially if they shorten your tour) as soon as you receive them.
  • Leave -- Don't take leave if you don't have enough balance to cover it.
  • UTAs -- If you don't perform UTAs consecutively, SGLI premiums can build up as debts on your next LES.

Members can use the Local Voucher in DTS to claim mileage for medical appointments. When doing so, they must use the official Defense Table of Official Distance (DTOD) for the mileage claimed.

The AFR FY25 IDT Travel Reimbursement Guide is available at: AFR FY25 IDT Travel Reimbursement Guide
